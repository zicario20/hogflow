# HogFlow Market Research — U.S. Pork Processing Market

This document records market hypotheses and preliminary research framing for HogFlow.

It is market research.

It is not technical validation.

It does not establish that HogFlow is commercially validated.

## 1. Research question

How many large U.S. pork-processing facilities may have an operational environment relevant to HogFlow's hypothesis of counting unique hogs moving through a constrained passage?

This document does not assume:

* any named company has a counting problem
* all facilities share the same workflow
* HogFlow is commercially viable

## 2. Market definition

HogFlow's initial target market is not every pork facility.

The broader pork industry includes materially different facility types, including:

* slaughter / harvest plants
* fresh pork processing plants
* further-processing plants
* bacon plants
* ham plants
* sausage plants
* packaged-meat plants
* cold storage
* distribution centers
* hog farms

The initial HogFlow target profile is:

A U.S. facility that receives live hogs and moves animals through a constrained physical flow before slaughter or primary processing.

Best-fit characteristics:

* live-hog receiving
* repeated animal movement through alleys or gates
* group or section-based movement
* a count-reconciliation use case
* feasible camera placement
* authorized ground-truth collection

Further-processing facilities should not automatically be included merely because they handle pork.

## 3. Facility qualification tiers

### Tier A — Confirmed or strongly supported live-hog processing candidate

Tier A means the available project research strongly supports that the facility is relevant to live-hog receiving, slaughter, or harvest operations consistent with HogFlow's initial market hypothesis.

### Tier B — Likely relevant, workflow requires verification

Tier B means the available project research suggests the facility may be relevant, but the actual workflow assumptions that matter to HogFlow still require facility-level validation.

### Tier C — Not an initial HogFlow target

Tier C means the available project research does not currently support inclusion in the initial HogFlow target market, either because the facility type is outside the hypothesis or because relevant live-hog workflow evidence is not established.

## 6. TAM, SAM, and SOM framework

### TAM — Total Addressable Market

TAM is all U.S. live-hog slaughter or harvest facilities where automated directional animal counting could theoretically be applicable.

Current status: Not yet calculated.

The 18–27 candidate range must not be treated as TAM.

### SAM — Serviceable Available Market

SAM requires qualification for:

* live-hog receiving
* compatible constrained animal flow
* feasible camera placement
* count-reconciliation use case
* legal and operational ability to run a pilot

The 18–27 range is a preliminary candidate universe among major processors.

It is not yet a validated SAM.

### SOM — Serviceable Obtainable Market

A hypothetical early-commercialization research scenario would be:

* 1 authorized pilot
* 1 successful reference facility
* 2–5 additional facilities with sufficiently similar workflows

Current status: Hypothetical scenario only.

## 8. Recommended market-validation order

### Priority 1 — Validate the problem

Research questions:

1. Is live-hog counting manually performed?
2. At what points?
3. How many separate counts occur?
4. What happens when counts disagree?
5. Is re-counting required?
6. Who owns the count?
7. Is the count recorded digitally?
8. What is the acceptable tolerance?
9. How frequently do animals reverse direction?
10. Are cameras already available at the relevant passage?

### Priority 2 — Validate workflow similarity

Compare the reference process against:

* another JBS pork plant
* one Smithfield harvest plant
* one Tyson pork plant
* one independent processor

The business hypothesis becomes stronger only if a similar counting need appears across multiple facilities.

### Priority 3 — Validate technical feasibility

Required technical-feasibility metrics:

* Exact Count Rate
* Mean Absolute Count Error
* Count Error Rate
* Undercount Rate
* Overcount Rate
* review-event frequency

This requires representative authorized video.

### Priority 4 — Validate economic value

Required value inputs:

* manual counting labor
* recount time
* discrepancy investigation time
* downtime only if verified
* hardware cost
* deployment cost
* support cost

## 9. Strategic recommendation

Do not present the market story as:

`There are thousands of pig companies.`

Use the more defensible statement:

`HogFlow is testing a narrow computer-vision hypothesis for live-hog count reconciliation in constrained animal passages. A preliminary market screen identifies approximately 18–27 candidate facilities among major U.S. pork processors, but facility workflow compatibility must be validated individually.`

If the workflow is highly specific to one plant, HogFlow may be a custom operational tool.

If similar workflows appear across multiple processors, HogFlow may have a repeatable vertical-market opportunity.

## 10. Market research risks

Relevant risks include:

* outdated public facility lists
* pork-processing facilities that do not receive live hogs
* different workflows within the same company
* corporate double-counting
* facility closures and replacements
* existing internal automation
* hidden operational systems
* technically successful counting without economic value
* biosecurity
* worker privacy
* animal welfare
* cybersecurity
* facility security
